Key — how to read it & how the 3rd-place teams are decided

1 · Reading a match box

  • Winner Group X — finished 1st in its group.
  • Runner-up Group X — finished 2nd.
  • Best 3rd Group A/B/… — one of the 8 qualifying 3rd-placed teams; the listed letters are the only groups whose 3rd can land in that slot.

The bracket splits into two halves: the top half (Matches feeding QF 97 & 98) leads to Semi-final 101; the bottom half (QF 99 & 100) leads to Semi-final 102. They only meet in the Final (M104).

2 · Which 3rd-placed teams continue (the calculation)

All 12 group runners-up auto-qualify; for 3rd place, the 12 third-placed teams are ranked against each other in a single table and the top 8 advance. Ranking order:

  1. Most points
  2. Best goal difference
  3. Most goals scored
  4. Best disciplinary / fair-play score (fewest yellow/red-card points)
  5. FIFA World Ranking at the start of the tournament

The bottom 4 third-placed teams are eliminated. (Head-to-head can't be used — these teams are in different groups.)

3 · Assigning the 8 thirds to the 8 winners — the eligibility matrix

Eight group winners (A, B, D, E, G, I, K, L) are each given a 3rd-placed opponent. A winner can never face a 3rd-placer from its own group, so each slot is restricted to 5 possible groups. This matrix is the actual rule behind the “A/B/C/D/F” labels — and the live populator above solves it as a one-to-one matching:

How the exact pairing is picked. Once the group stage ends, FIFA knows which 8 of the 12 groups produced a qualifying 3rd-placed team. That set of 8 group-letters is looked up in Annex C of the competition regulations, which lists all C(12,8) = 495 possible combinations and, for each, the one valid way to slot those 8 teams into the 8 winners above (a perfect one-to-one matching that respects the matrix and avoids group rematches). This page computes a valid such matching itself — for a given set of qualifying groups it may differ from FIFA’s exact Annex C row, but it always satisfies the same constraints.
